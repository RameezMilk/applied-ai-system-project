# Grooming for Dogs and Cats

Regular grooming prevents skin issues, matting, and excessive shedding.

**Dogs**:
- Short-haired (Beagle, Boxer, Lab): brush weekly, bathe every 4 to 8 weeks.
- Medium-haired (Golden Retriever, Border Collie): brush 2 to 3 times per week, bathe every 4 to 6 weeks.
- Long-haired or double-coated (Husky, Shih Tzu, Poodle): brush 4 to 7 times per week. Some breeds (Poodle, Doodle mixes) need professional grooming every 6 to 8 weeks.
- Nails: trim every 3 to 4 weeks. If you hear clicking on the floor, they're too long.
- Teeth: brush 2 to 3 times per week with dog-formulated toothpaste. Never use human toothpaste.

**Cats**:
- Short-haired: brush once per week.
- Long-haired (Persian, Maine Coon, Ragdoll): brush 3 to 5 times per week. Mats can form quickly.
- Most cats do not need baths unless they get into something or have a skin condition.
- Nails: trim every 2 to 4 weeks. Provide scratching posts to reduce furniture damage.

Brushing also strengthens the bond between owner and pet, and helps you spot lumps, ticks, or skin irritation early.
