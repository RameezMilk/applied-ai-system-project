# Pet Safety and Emergency Signs

This guide covers warning signs that require veterinary attention. PawPal+ is a scheduling assistant, not a veterinary service. Always contact a licensed veterinarian for medical concerns.

**Call a vet immediately if you observe**:
- Difficulty breathing, choking, or collapse
- Seizures or sudden loss of consciousness
- Bleeding that does not stop within a few minutes
- Suspected poisoning (chocolate, grapes, xylitol, lilies, antifreeze, human medications)
- Bloated, hard abdomen with unproductive retching (especially in large dogs — possible bloat / GDV)
- Repeated vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours
- Refusing food and water for more than 24 hours
- Sudden severe lethargy or hiding (especially in cats)
- Eye injury, sudden blindness, or pupils of unequal size
- Suspected broken bone or inability to put weight on a limb

**Pet poison hotlines (US)**:
- ASPCA Animal Poison Control: 888-426-4435 (fee may apply)
- Pet Poison Helpline: 855-764-7661 (fee may apply)

**Common household hazards**: chocolate, grapes/raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol (sugar-free gum and candy), lilies, antifreeze, rat poison, human pain medications (ibuprofen, acetaminophen), and certain houseplants.

When in doubt, call your vet or an emergency animal hospital. Do not wait.
