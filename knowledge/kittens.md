# Kitten Care (Under 1 Year)

Kittens have very different needs than adult cats. Their first year is a period of rapid growth and socialization.

**Feeding (8 weeks to 6 months)**: 3 to 4 meals per day of kitten-formula food. Kitten food is higher in protein and calories than adult food. Do not feed adult cat food to kittens under 1 year.

**Feeding (6 to 12 months)**: transition to 2 to 3 meals per day. Some breeds (Maine Coon, Ragdoll) should stay on kitten food until 18 to 24 months because they grow more slowly.

**Play**: kittens need 30 to 60 minutes of active play per day, broken into many short sessions (5 to 10 minutes each). Wand toys and small balls are ideal. Avoid using your hands as toys — it teaches biting.

**Sleep**: kittens sleep 16 to 20 hours per day. Frequent naps between play sessions are normal and healthy.

**Vet schedule**: vaccinations begin at 6 to 8 weeks and continue every 3 to 4 weeks until 16 weeks. Spay or neuter is typically done between 4 and 6 months.
