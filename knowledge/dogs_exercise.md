# Dog Exercise Guidelines

Adult dogs generally need 30 to 120 minutes of physical activity per day, depending on breed, age, and health.

- **Low-energy breeds** (Bulldogs, Basset Hounds, Pugs): 30 to 45 minutes daily, split into two short walks.
- **Medium-energy breeds** (Beagles, Cocker Spaniels, most mixed-breeds): 60 minutes daily, one walk plus active play.
- **High-energy breeds** (Border Collies, Huskies, Labradors, German Shepherds): 90 to 120 minutes daily, including running, fetch, or off-leash play.

Puppies under 12 months should not be over-exercised: a common rule of thumb is 5 minutes of structured exercise per month of age, twice a day. Senior dogs (7+ years) typically need shorter, gentler walks but still benefit from daily movement.

Always provide water before and after exercise. In hot weather (above 80°F / 27°C), walk early morning or late evening to avoid paw burns and heatstroke.
