# Puppy Care (Under 1 Year)

Puppies need structured routines for feeding, exercise, sleep, and house-training.

**Feeding**: 3 to 4 meals per day until 6 months, then 2 meals per day. Use puppy-formula food, which has more calories and DHA for brain development.

**Exercise**: the "5 minutes per month of age, twice a day" rule applies until growth plates close (around 12 to 18 months for most breeds, later for large breeds). A 4-month-old puppy = roughly 20 minutes of structured walking, twice daily. Free play in a yard is fine on top of this.

**Sleep**: puppies need 18 to 20 hours of sleep per day. Crate training supports this.

**House-training**: take a puppy out every 1 to 2 hours when awake, plus immediately after meals, naps, and play. Most puppies are reliably house-trained between 4 and 6 months.

**Socialization window**: 3 to 16 weeks is the critical socialization period. Expose puppies to many people, sounds, surfaces, and (vaccinated) dogs during this window to prevent fear-based behavior later.
