# Dog Feeding Schedule

Most adult dogs do best on two meals per day, spaced roughly 8 to 12 hours apart. Common pattern: breakfast around 7:00 to 8:00 AM and dinner around 5:00 to 6:00 PM.

- **Puppies (8 weeks to 6 months)**: 3 to 4 meals per day. Their small stomachs cannot hold a full day of calories at once.
- **Puppies (6 to 12 months)**: transition to 2 meals per day.
- **Adult dogs**: 2 meals per day is standard.
- **Senior dogs**: keep 2 meals per day; some dogs with dental or digestive issues may benefit from 3 smaller meals.

Avoid free-feeding (leaving food out all day) for most dogs — it makes weight management harder and disrupts house-training. Always provide fresh water.

Foods to never feed dogs: chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol (sugar substitute), macadamia nuts, alcohol, raw bread dough.
